The Tell Tale Heart

A narrator defends his sanity while describing the events of a murder he committed. He confesses to killing an old man, who caused the narrator no harm or wrongdoing, justifying the act because the old man's eye resembled a vulture's eye. The narrator continues to defend his sanity with the fact he took "careful" and "intricate" measures to commit this crime perfectly, down to hiding the old man's dismembered body parts under the floorboard. When the police come to the narrator's home to investigate, the narrator confuses his guilt as being able to hear the dead man's beating heart from beneath the floorboards, discouraging his sanity. The narrator confesses to the crime after convincing himself the officers could hear the heart beating.

The Maze Runner

Once a month, a teenager is sent into the Glade, trapped around a giant Maze that is set up with traps and seems impossible to escape through. Each person sent into the Glade has no memory from before they were sent in, nor do they know why they are there. Eventually, Thomas and the rest of those alive in the Glade go through dangerous measures to find an escape through the maze, all while figuring out who they really are and who put them there.
